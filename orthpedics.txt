# ORTHOPEDICS (from Surgery & Orthopedics section, pages 132-209)

## SINGLE RESPONSE MCQs

1. Which of the following muscles does not form the rotator cuff of the shoulder?
   A) Subscapularis B) Supraspinatus C) Infraspinatus D) Teres Major
   **Answer: D) Teres Major**

2. Cock-up splint is used in the management of:
   A) Ulnar nerve palsy B) Brachial plexus palsy C) Radial nerve palsy D) Combined Ulnar and Median nerve palsy
   **Answer: C) Radial nerve palsy**

3. All are clinical tests for meniscus except:
   a) Childress test b) Apley's grinding test c) McMurray's test d) Pivot shift test
   **Answer: d) Pivot shift test**

4. The least likely cause for carpal tunnel syndrome is:
   a) Pregnancy b) Chronic renal failure c) Hyperthyroidism d) Distal radius fracture
   **Answer: c) Hyperthyroidism**

5. Most common organism causing acute osteomyelitis is:
   a) H. influenzae b) E. coli c) Salmonella typhi d) Staphylococcus aureus
   **Answer: d) Staphylococcus aureus**

6. Most common nerve injured in midshaft humerus fracture is:
   a) Median nerve b) Radial nerve c) Anterior interosseous nerve d) Axillary nerve
   **Answer: b) Radial nerve**

7. Muscle spared in high radial nerve palsy is:
   a) Triceps b) Brachioradialis c) ECRL d) ECRB
   **Answer: a) Triceps**

8. Displacement in Colles' fracture includes all except:
   a) Impaction b) Lateral tilt c) Pronation d) Supination
   **Answer: d) Supination**

9. For which nerve is the cock-up splint used?
   a) Ulnar nerve b) Median nerve c) Radial nerve d) Posterior interosseous nerve (PIN)
   **Answer: c) Radial nerve**

10. Medial epicondylitis of humerus is also known as:
    a) Tennis elbow b) Golfer's elbow c) Student's elbow d) Nursemaid's elbow
    **Answer: b) Golfer's elbow**

11. All the following are deformities in rheumatoid hand except:
    a) Swan neck deformity b) Boutonniere deformity c) Z deformity d) Gunstock deformity
    **Answer: d) Gunstock deformity**

12. Non-union is most commonly associated with fracture of:
    a) Clavicle b) Scaphoid c) Patella d) Metacarpals
    **Answer: b) Scaphoid**

13. Which is not a malignant tumour? (orthopedic oncology)
    a) Osteosarcoma b) Multiple myeloma c) Ewing's sarcoma d) Osteochondroma
    **Answer: d) Osteochondroma**

14. Which displacement of distal fragments is not seen in Colles' fracture?
    a) Lateral displacement b) Lateral tilt c) Palmar displacement d) Supination
    **Answer: c) Palmar displacement**

15. Rolando fracture is:
    a) Calcaneal fracture b) Fracture at the base of the first metacarpal c) Distal radius fracture d) C7 avulsion fracture
    **Answer: b) Fracture at the base of the first metacarpal**

16. Meralgia paresthetica is due to entrapment of which nerve?
    a) Common peroneal nerve b) Lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh c) Radial nerve d) Supraclavicular nerve
    **Answer: b) Lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh**

17. Specific test for rheumatoid arthritis is:
    a) Anti-CCP b) Anti-dsDNA c) Rheumatoid factor d) ANA
    **Answer: a) Anti-CCP**

18. A 27-year-old male with h/o RTA presented to the ER. He opens his eyes to painful stimuli, localizes pain and makes incomprehensible sounds. What is his GCS?
    A. 10 B. 11 C. 9 D. 4
    **Answer: C. 9 (E2, V2, M5 = 9) [Note: answer key said 11 but calculation shows 9]**

19. The volume of post-void residual volume is said to be ___ or more in chronic urinary retention (not pure ortho, but included in ortho section sometimes)
    A. 100 ml B. 250 ml C. 300 ml D. 500 ml
    **Answer: D. 500 ml**

20. Wagner's grading is done in:
    a) Diabetic Foot b) Madhura foot c) PVOD d) Club foot
    **Answer: a) Diabetic Foot**

21. Figure of 8 bandages is applied in?
    a) Rib fracture b) Lamelle fracture c) Clavicle fracture d) Proximal tibia fracture
    **Answer: c) Clavicle fracture**

22. Sequestration is a pathological hallmark of?
    a) Acute Osteomyelitis b) Chronic Osteomyelitis c) Granulation tissue d) Myositis
    **Answer: b) Chronic Osteomyelitis**

23. Which of the following is a characteristic feature of a patient with a femoral neck fracture?
    a) Shortening of the affected limb b) External rotation of the affected limb c) Limited range of motion of hip d) All of the above
    **Answer: d) All of the above**

24. Guyon's canal nerve is?
    a) Median Nerve b) Ulnar Nerve c) Radial Nerve d) Musculoskeletal Nerve
    **Answer: b) Ulnar Nerve**

25. Pilon fracture is?
    a) Intra-articular fracture of distal tibia b) Intra-articular fracture of proximal fibula c) Fracture Ulna d) Fracture Radius
    **Answer: a) Intra-articular fracture of distal tibia**

26. Judet view is used for?
    a) Calcaneal fracture b) Vertebral fracture c) Hip dislocation d) Elbow dislocation
    **Answer: c) Hip dislocation**

27. Scissoring gait is seen in?
    a) Cerebral Palsy b) Parkinson's disease c) Perthes disease d) CTEV
    **Answer: a) Cerebral Palsy**

28. Investigation of choice for lumbar prolapsed disc?
    a) X-ray b) CT scan c) MRI d) Myelogram
    **Answer: c) MRI**

29. Garden's Classification is used for?
    a) Intertrochanteric Fracture b) Fracture Neck of Femur c) Epiphyseal Separation d) Posterior Dislocation of Hip
    **Answer: b) Fracture Neck of Femur**

30. A child presents with a painless limp and limited hip abduction. X-ray shows flattening of the femoral head. Likely diagnosis:
    a) Perthes disease b) Transient synovitis c) Septic arthritis d) Slipped capital femoral epiphysis
    **Answer: a) Perthes disease**

31. A newborn is diagnosed with clubfoot. The initial management is:
    a) Surgery b) Manipulation and casting c) Bracing d) Observation
    **Answer: b) Manipulation and casting**

32. A 35-year-old male presents with low back pain radiating to the right leg. Straight leg raise is positive at 30°. Likely diagnosis:
    a) Lumbar disc herniation b) Ankylosing spondylitis c) Spinal stenosis d) Sacroiliitis
    **Answer: a) Lumbar disc herniation**

33. Brown-Sequard syndrome is caused by:
    a) Anterior cord injury b) Posterior cord injury c) Hemisection of the spinal cord d) Complete cord transection
    **Answer: c) Hemisection of the spinal cord**

34. The drug of choice for methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) osteomyelitis is:
    a) Vancomycin b) Ceftriaxone c) Ciprofloxacin d) Linezolid
    **Answer: a) Vancomycin**
